    Leibniz Fast Calculation Competition – Terms of Participation

    1. Organiser of the Competition

    Leibniz Universität Hannover
    Welfengarten 1
    30167 Hannover, Germany
    Leibniz Universität Hannover is a legal entity under German public law and is legally represented by the President Prof. Dr. Volker Epping.

    2. Title of the Competition

    Leibniz Fast Calculation Competition 2025

    3. Subject of the Competition

    The competition focuses on innovations in molecular dynamics (MD) simulations, especially diffusion at interfaces. Projects should propose novel methods and technologies to enhance MD simulation speed and accuracy. This international competition fosters innovation, idea sharing, and collaboration among researchers.

    4. Period of Participation

    • Registration opens: Until September 15th, 2025, 23:59 CET
    • Working phase starts: September 16th, 2025
    • Submission deadline: January 20th, 2026, 23:59 CET

    5. Eligibility

    Open to researchers, scientists, and computational enthusiasts worldwide working on MD simulations. Submissions from contributors funded by SFB 1368 (DFG project number 394563137) are not eligible.

    • Only individual or team submissions allowed.
    • Teams must register via a team leader.
    • All members must consent to the terms.
    • Participants from countries restricted by law may be excluded.

    6. Participation and Acceptance of Terms

    Registration via web form is mandatory. By registering, participants accept these terms. All submissions must be independently created and properly cite any external sources.

    Participants agree to:

    • Store data on the organiser’s internal server.
    • Publish results (data) on orkg.org for broader accessibility.
    • License all data under CC BY SA 4.0.
    • Comply with FAIR data principles (Findable, Accessible, Interoperable, Reusable).

    7. Determination of the Winner

    Submissions will be evaluated based on:

    • Accuracy
    • Computational speed
    • Scalability
    • Innovation
    • Compliance with FAIR principles

    A jury will select the winners. Notification will be sent via email, and prize acceptance must be confirmed within four weeks.

    8. Prizes and Conditions

    1st Prize

    • Certificate of Excellence
    • 3-month stay as visiting researcher at an SFB sub-project
    • Monthly allowance: €1300
    • Travel allowance per DAAD table
    • Open Access publication cost coverage with the subproject leader

    2nd Prize

    • Certificate of Excellence
    • 1-month stay as visiting researcher
    • €1300 allowance
    • Travel reimbursement per DAAD table

    3rd Prize

    • Certificate of Excellence

    Visiting Researcher Conditions

    • Subject to successful visa (if required)
    • Valid health, accident, and liability insurance is required in Germany
    • Participants must comply with German tax and social security laws

    9. Award Ceremony

    Details will be announced later. The 1st prize winner may present their solution. Travel and accommodation will be covered for German participants. International participants can join via web meeting.
